# BPH Sanctions & Enforcement Timeline

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-16
**Maintainer:** CrimsonVector Research

---

> **Purpose:** Chronological record of all sanctions, law enforcement operations, provider lifecycle events, and evasion responses related to Bulletproof Hosting (BPH) and Threat Activity Enabler (TAE) networks. Entries are in reverse-chronological order (newest first) for rapid analyst reference.

> **Legend:**
> - **Sanctions** -- Designation by OFAC, EU, UK, AU, or other authority
> - **LE Operation** -- Law enforcement seizure, takedown, or arrest
> - **Provider Event** -- Incorporation, registration, dissolution, or transfer of hosting entity or AS resource
> - **Evasion** -- Rebranding, prefix migration, front-company creation, or other sanctions/LE circumvention
> - **Intelligence** -- Publication of key research, reports, or disclosures

## 2025

**2025-12** | **Sanctions** | **Canada / 764 Network**
> Canada designates the 764 network (including Maniac Murder Cult and Terrorgram Collective) as a terrorist entity under Canadian anti-terrorism law, marking one of the first state-level terrorist designations for an online extremist network with ties to BPH-hosted infrastructure.

**2025-11-25** | **LE Operation** | **Cryptomixer.io**
> Cryptomixer.io seized in a joint Switzerland-Germany operation. The mixer had processed EUR 1.3B in Bitcoin since 2016. EUR 25M+ in cryptocurrency confiscated and 12TB of transaction data seized, providing investigators with extensive financial trail data for ongoing BPH-related money laundering investigations.

**2025-11-19** | **Sanctions** | **OFAC/UK/AU -- Media Land LLC + Yalishanda (Alexander Volosovyk)**
> Coordinated trilateral sanctions action. OFAC, UK, and Australia sanction Media Land LLC, its operator Yalishanda (Alexander Volosovyk), three additional leaders, and three sister companies. Media Land operated as a major BPH provider supporting ransomware, fraud, and botnet infrastructure.

**2025-11-19** | **Sanctions** | **OFAC/UK -- Hypercore LTD + Datavice (Aeza Fronts)**
> OFAC and UK sanction Hypercore LTD (identified as an Aeza front company) and Datavice (Aeza expansion entity), continuing the coordinated pressure campaign against the Aeza hosting ecosystem following CEO Penzev's arrest.

**2025-11-19** | **Intelligence** | **CISA/NSA/FBI/DC3 + Five Eyes -- BPH Guidance**
> CISA, NSA, FBI, DC3, and Five Eyes partners publish "Bulletproof Defense: Mitigating Risks from Bulletproof Hosting Providers" -- the first comprehensive interagency guidance document on identifying and defending against BPH-sourced threats.

**2025-11-13** | **LE Operation** | **Operation Endgame Phase 2**
> Operation Endgame targets Rhadamanthys, VenomRAT, and Elysium botnet infrastructure. 1,025 servers taken down worldwide. VenomRAT suspect arrested in Greece. Represents continued momentum from the May 2025 Endgame operations.

**2025-11-12** | **LE Operation** | **CrazyRDP**
> CrazyRDP seized by Dutch police. Approximately 250 physical servers located in The Hague and Zoetermeer confiscated. AS394711 (Limenet LLC) and AS211252 (Delis LLC) taken offline. The service had provided RDP access enabling ransomware operators and fraud networks.

**2025-11-06** | **Intelligence** | **Recorded Future Insikt / aurologic GmbH**
> Recorded Future Insikt publishes "Malicious Infrastructure Finds Stability with aurologic GmbH" -- names aurologic as a central upstream hub for malicious hosting, documenting its role as a key connectivity provider for multiple downstream BPH networks.

**2025-10** | **LE Operation** | **SIM Box CaaS Network**
> SIM box Communications-as-a-Service network takedown. Seven Latvian nationals arrested. 1,200+ SIM-box devices and 40,000 SIM cards seized. The network had facilitated 49M+ online account registrations used in fraud, identity theft, and cybercrime operations.

**2025-10** | **Intelligence** | **ShinyHunters / Salesforce Campaign**
> ShinyHunters social engineering campaign siphons 1B+ Salesforce customer records, demonstrating the scale of data theft operations enabled by BPH-hosted command-and-control and exfiltration infrastructure.

**2025-09** | **Provider Event** | **LockBit 5.0 Released**
> LockBit 5.0 released at approximately EUR 400 / $500 per license, with variants targeting Windows, Linux, and VMware ESXi environments. Demonstrates the resilience of ransomware operations despite the February 2024 Operation Cronos takedown.

**2025-09** | **Provider Event** | **DragonForce/LockBit/Qilin Coalition**
> DragonForce, LockBit, and Qilin announce coalition on dark web forums, signaling consolidation among ransomware-as-a-service operations and shared BPH infrastructure dependencies.

**2025-08-25** | **Provider Event** | **SLSH Alliance**
> SLSH alliance announced -- a collaboration between Scattered Spider, LAPSUS$, and ShinyHunters threat groups, representing a convergence of social engineering, data extortion, and BPH-dependent operations.

**2025-08-14** | **Sanctions** | **OFAC -- Garantex/Grinex/Exved Expansion**

**2025-07-16** | **LE Operation** | **Operation Eastwood -- NoName057(16)**
> NoName057(16) hacktivist infrastructure disrupted in Operation Eastwood. 19 countries and 200 police officers participated. 100+ servers seized. However, the network quickly recovers capability, illustrating the resilience of distributed DDoS-for-hire operations hosted on BPH infrastructure.

**2025-07-01** | **Sanctions** | **OFAC/UK NCA -- Aeza International Ltd**
> OFAC designates Aeza International Ltd in coordination with UK NCA. CEO Penzev, General Director Bozoyan, CTO Gast, and 33% owner Knyazev personally sanctioned. A TRON wallet is designated, marking one of the first cryptocurrency wallet-specific sanctions tied to a BPH provider.

**2025-06-24** | **Evasion** | **WorkTitans B.V. AS209847**

### Key Analytical Pattern

> **Average evasion lead time: Negative.** In every major case tracked in 2025, successor entities were established *before* sanctions or LE actions took effect. The Stark Industries case is paradigmatic: UFO Hosting was registered 68 days before EU sanctions, PQ Hosting Plus 7 days before, and the corporate rebrand to THE.Hosting occurred the day before announcement. This pattern indicates that sanctioned entities have consistent advance warning of enforcement actions, enabling pre-positioned evasion infrastructure. Effective enforcement requires targeting the successor entities simultaneously with the original designation -- as demonstrated by OFAC's expanded Garantex re-designation in August 2025, which named Grinex, Exved, and related entities in a single action.
